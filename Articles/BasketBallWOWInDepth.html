Basketball W.O.W. In Depth

Published on: 27/10/2023
Written by Hank Baxter

Basketball is one of the most popular W.O.W. (Well-being on Wednesday) activities offered in the school, and basketball is liked for a reason. It offers: fun, a workout and a chance to mess around with your friends. Basketball teaches skills, teamwork and allows people to have fun playing against each other learning good sportsmanship which is needed. The teacher that supervises basketball, Mr Reihill says that “the basketball W.O.W. is going great and that nearly 30 people are coming in every week”. He also says that “he hopes to enter the teams to national and regional competitions next year,”. He says that “they get to play matches almost every single week and they also help teach people how to play basketball”.

We interviewed a member of the Basketball W.O.W., Brajan Tanski, about his thoughts on the W.O.W. He says “I enjoy it, but I would like to play in the gym a little bit more,” This is understandable as wet clothes and basketball aren't exactly a good match. Another member, Vaughan Campbell, agreed with Brajan’s opinion on playing in the gym. It is clear that the basketball players feel they need to play indoors more. Vaughan is pleased about the fact that the school is taking priority in students' wellbeing and allowing us to do these activities, which we can all agree on.

Having over 30 students every week can make creating fair teams somewhat hard, but there are some different groups, such as the junior group and the senior group, so no need to worry about getting smacked halfway across the court by a fifth year that didnt see you! The teachers will allow you to join whichever group you want, but try to make an educated guess about where you belong.
