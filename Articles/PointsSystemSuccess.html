Has the Point System been successful?

Published on: 23/01/2023
Written by Calum Locke

If you are a member of the Sancta Maria College school community, you have likely encountered the points system in some way. As it has been introduced only recently, many people may find it confusing, or they may simply resent the system. If you wish to better understand the points system, this article is for you. In this article I will be giving a general overview of what the points system is, its purpose, its benefits and flaws as well as the improvements we have seen so far as a result of it.

The first thing I would like to mention is the purpose of the points system. The points system is a transparent disciplinary system which rewards students for good behaviour, while sanctioning bad behaviour. You are given points when you perform well in an area of school life, and deducted points when you fail to abide by the school rules. Each student’s points are visible on the VS Ware portal, which can be accessed by parents and teachers. This allows students and their parents to view their points in real time. This gives parents a general idea on their student’s performance and behaviour at school.

A huge benefit of this system is the transparency of it. When a student is deducted points, the reason must be clear and they must be made aware of it. There are several categories of behaviour for which students can be awarded or deducted points, with more categories assigned for awarding points. Another benefit is that it promotes good behaviour. Students will generally feel more encouraged to be more involved in classwork and to participate in school activities, as they will be rewarded. The points system also allows for clearer boundaries between good and bad behaviour, and gives students more clarity on the punishments they will receive. Due to an increase in clarity surrounding discipline, the points system can also improve the relationship between students and teachers, as it gives teachers an easy way to enforce rules in a transparent way. Classes can be more constructive as there is clear punishment for misbehaviour, so conflict between students and teachers is easier to avoid punishment for misbehaviour, so conflict between students and teachers is easier to avoid.

A flaw of the points system, which was much more evident when it was first introduced, is that there can be a lack of consistency around how points are given. Students often complained that teachers never failed to deduct points for bad behaviour, but some students would not be given points despite making a strong effort and producing high quality work. The school addressed this and added more categories for good behaviour, and has proven to be more successful. The points system’s aim is not purely to sanction students. It is designed to be as an incentive for good behaviour, and to make discipline more transparent.

In conclusion, the points system is a transparent disciplinary system aimed to encourage good behaviour. Teachers can deduct and award points with a much stronger focus on fairness and clarity than ever before. If you remember one thing from this article, make it the fact that the points system was not created to punish students or to make their lives more difficult. It simply aims to encourage good behaviour and to create a more constructive and positive school environment.
