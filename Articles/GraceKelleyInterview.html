SMC Students Get To Interview Grace Kelley

Published on: 27/10/2023
Written by Maya Nimhorain


Grace Kelley

Life and Career
Grace Kelley was born and grew up here in Mayo. She attended Sancta Maria College then followed her passion and went on to earn a degree in Drama from Trinity and a M. Phil in children's literature and children’s literature in Trinity College, as well as studying acting, drama, theatre and English literature in various other college courses.

She has acted in multiple film productions mainly comprising Shakespeare renditions, short films and a small appearance in the feature film ‘My Sailor My Love’.
She is a voracious reader and writer and has written several pieces of flash fiction and short stories, her favourite genre of books is historical fiction with a fantasy twist and her best-loved author is Angela Carter.

Paper Lanterns
A long with 2 other writers, Grace founded Paper Lanterns Teen and Young Adult literary journal in 2020. Paper Lanterns acts as a platform to promote the voices of young people in Ireland and across the world, providing new and exciting content for our youth. As well as being perfect for enthusiasts of teen and YA literature. The subject matter comprises mainly of art, flash fiction, features, book reviews and interviews.

Grace advice for writing a book is to most importantly keep breathing, be mindful, let your creativity flow, re-read your treasured books, and analyse the reason of your partiality towards them, connect with your favourite authors and lastly to join a creative writing class, to have a community to support and encourage you.

Symbolism
The unusual name Paper Lanterns symbolises the teenage years. Paper - the amount of knowledge it can gather, how impressionable it is, and yet paper is a reminder of natural growth, it can be made from trees, bamboo, cotton and plants. Even the largest trees grow and flourish from a small seed.
Lantern - Fundamentally, the lantern provides light. The act of igniting a fire is a tale as old as time, and so is the journey from teenage hood to adulthood. Light in its context helps lead the way and pushes aside the surrounding darkness. Where the long path before us is useless without the glow of a lantern to show us the way. The adolescent years will always be an illumination in your memories.
